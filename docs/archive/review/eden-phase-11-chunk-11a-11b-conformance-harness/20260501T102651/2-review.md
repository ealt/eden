**Findings**

1. Chapter 09 still has an internal contradiction about what unqualified “v0 conformance” means. [plan L851](</Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-11-chunk-11a-11b-conformance-harness.md:851>) defines a v0-conforming IUT as implementing chapters 03 and 06 as well, but [plan L860](</Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-11-chunk-11a-11b-conformance-harness.md:860>) says an IUT may claim the level it passes and that “v0 conformance” without a qualifier means the highest level shipped at the time. When only `v1` has shipped, those two statements conflict: the highest shipped level does not yet cover 03/06. [plan L975](</Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-11-chunk-11a-11b-conformance-harness.md:975>) repeats the same ambiguity for the reference impl. The clean fix is to require level-qualified claims until `v1+roles+integrator` exists, or reserve unqualified “v0 conformance” for that final level only.

2. The composite-commit section is still internally inconsistent. [plan L640](</Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-11-chunk-11a-11b-conformance-harness.md:640>) says “six composite-commit cases,” but the table now has eight rows, and [plan L655](</Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-11-chunk-11a-11b-conformance-harness.md:655>) includes `test_evaluate_terminal_eval_error_keeps_trial_starting`, which is explicitly the non-composite control case, not a `05 §2.2` composite commit. The new retry-exhausted `trial.eval_errored` case is the real `§2.2` addition and is correct; the stale framing around the section should be cleaned up so the scenario bucket matches the spec category.

3. A few stale phrases still reintroduce the old overclaim. [plan L358](</Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-11-chunk-11a-11b-conformance-harness.md:358>) says “Every conforming v0 IUT must pass,” but the rest of the revision now correctly says `v1` is only the task-store/wire subset. [plan L134](</Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-11-chunk-11a-11b-conformance-harness.md:134>) still says “§4.3 at-least-once via replay,” even though the scenario was correctly changed to reconnect-based subscribe delivery. These are small, but they are exactly the kind of stale wording that later turns into spec drift.

**Assessment**

The round-1 substantive problems are mostly fixed. The `trial.eval_errored` gap is addressed, `already-exists` is now observable, the at-least-once test is pointed at the right surface, the adapter/auth text is materially better, and the subscribe-timeout operational issue is handled.

What remains is mostly consistency cleanup, plus the important chapter-09 ambiguity about unqualified “v0 conformance.” I would fix that before implementation, because it affects what the new spec chapter is actually claiming.
