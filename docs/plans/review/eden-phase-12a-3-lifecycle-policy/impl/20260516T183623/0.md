## Load-bearing decisions to review

1. **Termination is the orchestrator's 5th decision type, consulted FIRST.**
   Per plan §3.0 the alternative (separate "experiment-lifecycle gate" outside §6.2)
   was considered and rejected; check the spec text in chapter 03 §6.2.
2. **`dispatch_mode.termination` defaults to "manual".** Pre-12a-3 deployments
   have no termination policy; defaulting "manual" preserves their behavior.
3. **Four legacy fields removed from normative schema** (no compat shim).
   Greenfield posture per AGENTS.md.
4. **`update_experiment_state` is NOT a public wire op.** v0 has exactly one legal
   lifecycle transition; a generic state mutator would expose surface for future
   transitions that don't exist. Only `POST /terminate` is public.
5. **Multi-instance terminate is idempotent — first reason wins.**
6. **`StoreClient.read_experiment` synthesizes `created_at`** from client wall-clock
   (no `GET /experiment` endpoint in v0). Wall-time policies via wire don't work;
   documented as known degradation.
7. **The §6.4.1 race contract.** `terminate_experiment` vs `integrate_variant`:
   event ordering NOT pinned; cardinality + final state ARE pinned.
8. **Citation discipline.** Each conformance test's citation falls within its
   declared CONFORMANCE_GROUP's chapter-9 §5 entry. Some wave-6 tests landed in
   `test_experiment_lifecycle.py` (rather than `test_termination_decision.py`)
   because their citations (e.g. §2.9) are in the lifecycle group's declared set.
