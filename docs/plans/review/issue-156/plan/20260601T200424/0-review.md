**Feasibility**

Assessment: the reference-adapter mechanics are implementable, but the current level/contract story does not compose cleanly.

- `crash_restart` cannot be both optional and part of mandatory `v1` coverage. Chapter 9 defines conformance levels by scenario coverage, and the durability row sits in the `v1` table: [09-conformance.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden-worktrees/plan-issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance/spec/v0/09-conformance.md:32), [09-conformance.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden-worktrees/plan-issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance/spec/v0/09-conformance.md:44). That defeats the stated gap. This needs to become mandatory for adapters used to claim `v1`, or move to a separate level, or fail the run when the `v1` durability group is skipped.

The plan’s scenario 5 and capstone snapshot require `GET /dispatch_mode`: [plan](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden-worktrees/plan-issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance/docs/plans/issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance.md:266), [plan](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden-worktrees/plan-issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance/docs/plans/issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance.md:507). Until that is tightened, dispatch-mode durability is not a universal `v1` assertion.
- The reference-adapter mechanics themselves are otherwise sound. File-backed SQLite plus SIGKILL plus port rebind plus `WireClient.copy_worker_bearers_from(...)` is a workable composition with the current adapter/plugin/client shapes: [adapter.py](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden-worktrees/plan-issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance/conformance/src/conformance/harness/adapter.py:18), [reference adapter](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden-worktrees/plan-issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance/conformance/src/conformance/adapters/reference/adapter.py:24), [plugin.py](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden-worktrees/plan-issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance/conformance/src/conformance/harness/plugin.py:237), [wire_client.py](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden-worktrees/plan-issue-156-experiment-durability-conformance/conformance/src/conformance/harness/wire_client.py:65). The blocker is not the subprocess mechanics; it is the conformance contract around them.

I would stop here for this round. The feasibility issues change the level boundary and the required adapter contract, so alternatives/completeness/edge-case review would be premature.

Overall assessment: the plan is strong on scoping the wire-observable projection and on distinguishing durability from checkpoint round-trip, but it is not ready yet. It needs to resolve whether durability is truly mandatory at `v1`, and whether `dispatch_mode` is actually part of the required `v1` read surface.
