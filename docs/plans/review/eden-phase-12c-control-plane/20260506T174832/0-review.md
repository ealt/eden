I don’t see a standalone missing-context blocker that should stop the review before design analysis. The first substantive issues are at the **feasibility** level, so I’m stopping there.

**Findings**
- The plan does not reconcile the control-plane registry with the current experiment-bound data plane. [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:205) lines 205-212 define registry entries as `{experiment_id, config_uri, last_known_state, lease}`, and [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:279) lines 279-283 say the UI enumerates experiments and then calls “each task-store-server in turn”, but the scope section simultaneously says `eden_storage.Store` is unchanged at [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:570). The current reference impl is still single-experiment at construction time: [reference/services/task-store-server/src/eden_task_store_server/app.py](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/reference/services/task-store-server/src/eden_task_store_server/app.py:21) lines 21-57 build one `Store` from one `experiment_id`, and [reference/packages/eden-wire/src/eden_wire/client.py](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/reference/packages/eden-wire/src/eden_wire/client.py:71) lines 71-84 bake one `experiment_id` into every client. As written, the plan never chooses between “shared multi-tenant task-store-server” and “one task-store-server per experiment with registry-held endpoint metadata”, so the orchestrator/UI routing story is not executable.

- Lease authority contradicts the 12a orchestrator identity model. The orchestrator flow in [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:287) lines 287-323 has the orchestrator worker acquiring, renewing, and releasing leases, and [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:611) lines 611-614 say the control plane reuses the same admin-token plus worker-credential model as 12a-1. But [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:641) makes all control-plane ops, including acquire/renew/release, “Authority: admin-token.” That either means every orchestrator replica must hold deployment-admin power forever, or the documented orchestrator flow cannot work. It also regresses the 12a-2 boundary where the orchestrator bootstraps with admin and then uses its own worker credential for ongoing operations: [eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md:553) lines 553-573.

- The control-plane partition story is internally inconsistent, and in the documented flow it can produce unbounded dual ownership. In the renewal thread, a `TransportError` only logs and retries at [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:303) lines 303-310; the replica drops ownership only on `LeaseNotHeld`. But the hand-off section claims the overlap window is bounded by `lease_duration / 3` at [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:342) lines 342-360, and the risks section says control-plane downtime makes replicas “eventually drop” their held experiments at [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:895) lines 895-902. Those cannot all be true. If A can still reach the task store but cannot renew, B can acquire after expiry while A keeps dispatching indefinitely because it never gets `LeaseNotHeld`. That is precisely the split-brain case the lease layer is supposed to bound.

- Import-plus-register atomicity is self-contradictory, and the partial-success path leaves the experiment undiscoverable by the new orchestrator model. Decision 9 says import is “atomic with the import” at [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:170) lines 170-176, but §3.5 immediately says registration failure is a partial success with warnings at [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:427) lines 427-437, and §7.2 explicitly says true atomicity is not provided at [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:803) lines 803-820. The plan needs one contract, not all three. This matters operationally because the orchestrator discovers work via `list_experiments()` in [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:294) lines 294-299 and 327-334, so a partially registered import is not just “missing from dashboards”; it is also invisible to lease acquisition.

- The lease hand-off safety claim overstates what 12a-2 actually guarantees. [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:163) lines 163-167 make one lease cover all five decisions, and [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:356) lines 356-360 say the hand-off race is handled because writes serialize via Store CAS. That is not true for `ideation_creation`. 12a-2 explicitly split decisions into exact-idempotent vs bounded-overshoot classes: [eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md:255) lines 255-277. So 12a-2 is a valid safety net for execution/evaluation/integration/termination, but not for the blanket “either A wins or B wins via CAS” description in §3.3.

- Decision 7’s claimed scaling behavior is not delivered by the proposed acquisition loop, and the worker-identity handling regresses 12a-2. [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:160) lines 160-167 say `N` replicas and `M` experiments yield up to `min(N, M)` parallel loops, but the startup and acquisition threads in [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:294) lines 294-299 and 327-334 are greedy and non-rebalancing: the first replica can acquire every experiment and keep them forever. On top of that, §3.2 hardcodes `worker_id = "auto-orchestrator-<host>"` at line 291, while the file plan for the CLI drops the 12a-2 `--worker-id` control and only mentions `--control-plane-url`, `--lease-duration-seconds`, and optional `--experiment-id` at [eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12c-control-plane.md:663). That conflicts with 12a-2’s explicit multi-replica rule that operators supply unique worker IDs per replica: [eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md:555) lines 555-578, grounded in the 12a-1 worker-id uniqueness contract at [eden-phase-12a-1-worker-identity.md](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-1-worker-identity.md:89) lines 89-107.

**Overall Assessment**
This is not ready to execute as written. The core control-plane topology, lease authority, lease-failure semantics, and import/register contract need to be made internally consistent first; until those are settled, reviewing alternatives or completeness would be premature.
