**Stopped At Level 5: Edge Cases**

- The plan still does not define the contract for a `dispatch_mode` flip that races with an already-running orchestrator iteration. `dispatch_mode` is explicitly mutable at [§3.2](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md:284), and the reference loop is described as reading it “at iteration start” at [§5.6](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md:777). [§7.7](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md:1148) covers operator-vs-operator toggle races, but not the more important operator-vs-orchestrator case: the orchestrator reads `evaluation_dispatch=auto`, the operator flips it to `manual`, and the orchestrator is about to issue `create_task(kind=evaluation)`. The plan needs to say whether the flip takes effect only on the next iteration, or whether each decision re-checks `dispatch_mode` immediately before acting. Right now the manual-mode guarantee is underspecified.

- Group-membership revocation of an authenticated orchestrator mid-iteration is still unaddressed. The authority model says `accept` / `reject` / `integrate_variant` / execution-task creation require `orchestrators` membership at [§3.7](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md:527), and §5.4 says those checks happen server-side on each request at [§5.4](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md:760). But the plan never states what the orchestrator loop should do if its worker is removed from `orchestrators` after startup and starts getting 403s in the middle of a pass. Should it log-and-skip one op, retry next iteration, or exit hard because its role authority has been revoked? Since you explicitly called out “removed from the orchestrators group mid-iteration,” this needs an intentional answer and at least one test.

- `reassign_task` under target/group churn is still not pinned. The operation is defined at [§3.6](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-2-orchestrator-as-role.md:488), and 12a-1’s claim semantics resolve target eligibility at claim time, not assignment time, at [12a-1 D.3](/Users/ericalt/Documents/eden/docs/plans/eden-phase-12a-1-worker-identity.md:267). But the plan never says whether `reassign_task(new_target=...)` must validate that the target worker/group exists at the moment of reassignment, nor what should happen if the target worker is removed from that group immediately afterward. That is exactly the “reassign_task targeting a worker who is themselves about to be removed” case you asked about. If the intended semantics are “claim-time eligibility only; reassignment may legally become stale immediately,” say that explicitly and test it. If not, the plan needs a stronger atomic precondition.

Completeness is now in much better shape. Once those three edge-case contracts are spelled out, this plan should be in solid review shape.
